What are imperatives?

Imperatives are commands, instructions, advice or invitations. They usually use the base form of the verb.

  • Positive: Recycle paper.
  • Negative: Do not burn trash.
  • Polite classroom use: Please listen carefully.

Imperative Structure

TypeStructureExample
AffirmativeBase verb + complementProtect the river.
NegativeDo not / Don't + base verbDon't throw trash here.
Polite requestPlease + base verbPlease save water.
SequenceVerb + detailsClean the park every week.

From Description to Action

To be

The river is dirty.

The street is dangerous.

Imperative

Clean the river.

Be careful on the street.

Imperatives for Community Care

  • Keep the park clean.
  • Protect the water.
  • Plant a tree.
  • Recycle plastic and paper.
  • Use public transport.
  • Help your community.
  • Do not litter.
  • Do not burn trash.

Imperatives for Products and Services

Promotion

  • Buy fresh bread.
  • Try this local product.
  • Choose good quality fruit.
  • Visit our community market.

Service messages

  • Call for delivery.
  • Ask for repair service.
  • Compare the prices.
  • Support local businesses.
