Unit 2 Grammar Presentation

There is / There are

Learn how to describe environmental problems and risks in a place using there is and there are. This presentation includes explanations, examples, and practice exercises.

Risk vocabulary

pollution, trash, wildfire, fire, flood, danger, clean air, safe places

Main goal

Describe the existence of problems or conditions in a community.

Part 1

What is the difference?

There is = singular noun or uncountable noun
There are = plural noun
There is a wildfire near the forest.
There is pollution in the air.
There are many problems in the community.
There are safe places in the city.
Part 2

Affirmative sentences

There is + singular / uncountable noun
There are + plural noun
StructureExample
There is + singular nounThere is a fire in the forest.
There is + uncountable nounThere is trash in the river.
There are + plural nounThere are dangerous places in the town.
Part 3

Negative sentences

There is not (isn't)
There are not (aren't)
There isn't clean water in the area.
There isn't a safe road near the river.
There aren't safe places during the wildfire.
There aren't enough trees in the park.
Part 4

Questions

Is there + singular / uncountable noun?
Are there + plural noun?
Is there pollution in the community? — Yes, there is.
Is there a fire near the mountain? — No, there isn't.
Are there floods in this region? — Yes, there are.
Are there safe places here? — No, there aren't.
Part 5

Vocabulary in context

pollutiontrashwildfirefire flooddangerclean airsafe places
There is pollution near the factory.
There is trash on the street.
There are floods during the rainy season.
There are safe places in the school.
Part 6

Useful tips

  • Use there is for one thing: There is a fire.
  • Use there are for two or more things: There are many risks.
  • Use is there...? and are there...? for questions.
  • Use isn't and aren't for short negative forms.
Exercise 1

Choose the correct answer

1. ___ a wildfire near the forest.
2. ___ many dangerous places in the city.
Exercise 2

Complete the sentences

1. There ______ pollution in the air.
2. There ______ floods in the region.
3. ______ there a fire near the mountain?
Exercise 3

Change to negative

1. There is a safe place. → There isn't a safe place.

2. There are clean streets. → There aren't clean streets.

3. There is water in the river. → There isn't water in the river.

Final practice

Write your own sentences

  • Write 2 affirmative sentences with there is / there are.
  • Write 1 negative sentence.
  • Write 1 question about risks in your community.
Example: There is trash on the street. There are dangerous places near the river.

Excellent! Now you can describe risks and environmental problems in English.
